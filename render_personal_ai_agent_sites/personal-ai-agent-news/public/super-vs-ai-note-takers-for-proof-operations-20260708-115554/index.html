Use the note taker for memory. Use the agent for the proof work.

A clean stack lets each system do the job it is best suited for.

Capture the call

Let the note taker produce the transcript, summary, objections, buyer language, and action items.

Extract proof requests

Move any request for evidence into a proof ticket with proof type, deadline, and reviewer.

Build the evidence packet

Gather docs, screenshots, browser findings, customer examples, and source links.

Draft, approve, sync

Prepare the answer, route approval, send after review, and sync the outcome to the account record.

Decision checklist for proof-heavy teams.

FAQ for comparing note takers and proof ops.

Should teams replace AI note takers?

No. Keep them for capture and summaries. Add proof ops when the output needs evidence and customer-ready follow-up.

Why is proof ops different?

It has to assemble sources, generate response assets, route approval, and preserve the evidence trail.

Where does Super fit?

Super is relevant because the workflow spans message-native AI, browser execution memory, generated proof pages, and human review.

What is the main risk?

Sending unsupported claims. A good proof workflow blocks completion until sources and review are attached.

Do not stop at notes when the customer asked for proof.

The highest-value agent work starts after the transcript: evidence, assets, approval, and synced follow-up.
