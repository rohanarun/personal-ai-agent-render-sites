A room for proving autonomy before granting it.

Personal AI agents become valuable when they do work without constant supervision. They become risky when that autonomy arrives without a review surface. A rehearsal room gives operators a disciplined way to ask: if this promoted rule had been active, what would the agent have done, where would it have failed, and what proof would remain?

What rehearsal room software includes

The room gathers the evidence needed to promote or reject a candidate rule. It is not only a log viewer. It is a QA surface for future agent behavior.

  • Approval history with edits, rejections, reversals, and context.
  • Backtests that show where the rule would have skipped approval.
  • Shadow-mode predictions while humans still decide.
  • Receipt previews before automatic actions begin.

Buyer checklist

Use this checklist to separate a real rehearsal room from a prettier audit log.

1
Backtests are explicit.

The room shows what would have happened under the candidate rule.

2
Negative examples are visible.

Near misses, edits, rejected proposals, and reversals are included in the review.

3
Freshness is enforced.

The promoted rule expires or asks again when context changes.

4
Receipts are previewed.

The operator sees the proof trail before any approval is skipped.

FAQ

Is rehearsal room software the same as an audit log?

No. Audit logs explain what happened after the fact. Rehearsal rooms test what would happen before autonomy is granted.

What risk guidance applies?

The framing aligns with the NIST AI Risk Management Framework and LLM application risk categories described by the OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications.

When should a rule stay in approval?

Keep it in approval when evidence is thin, misses are high-impact, context gets stale quickly, or the receipt would not explain the automatic action clearly.
