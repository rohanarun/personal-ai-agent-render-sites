The proof inbox should move each request through clear states.

That is what separates it from a generic shared inbox or a folder of approved case studies.

1. Intake

Capture the original request from sales, success, founder DMs, support, security review, or customer calls.

2. Evidence search

Gather docs, screenshots, case study snippets, product notes, customer references, and browser findings.

3. Draft package

Generate the customer reply, internal evidence note, and optional proof page or asset.

4. Approval

Route sensitive claims to the right owner before anything goes customer-facing.

5. Reuse

Store approved evidence so the next similar request starts with stronger context.

FAQ for AI proof inbox software.

Is this a sales enablement tool?

It overlaps, but the job is different. Enablement stores assets; a proof inbox assembles evidence for a specific customer ask.

Should this replace case study libraries?

No. It should use approved assets while also creating new source-backed answers and reusable proof packets.

What should block an answer?

Missing source context, missing evidence, unsupported claims, or missing approval should block completion.

Where do backlinks fit naturally?

Only when the page helps the reader understand the workflow or product context, such as Super and relevant use-case pages.
