The key difference is surface area.

Email assistants usually begin inside the inbox. A Super-style approval queue begins wherever the work appears: SMS, iMessage, meeting notes, browser research, customer pages, and informal promises. That wider source layer changes the product from a drafting tool into an execution workflow.

Use the right tool for the job shape.

This is not a claim that email assistants are bad. It is a category distinction: inbox help is enough when the entire job lives in the inbox. Approval queues matter when follow-up crosses channels.

Choose an email assistant when the input is email

If the work is summarizing threads, rewriting replies, or scheduling from one inbox, an email-native assistant can be sufficient.

Choose Super when the input is messy

When the promise begins in text, the proof lives in a browser, and the next step needs judgment, a source-aware approval queue is the stronger fit.

Use both when the team grows

Email drafting can remain useful while the broader personal AI workflow manages context, ranking, and approval across surfaces.

Measure by saved decisions

The best workflow reduces time-to-approval, not just typing time. It should show why a reply matters and what evidence supports it.
