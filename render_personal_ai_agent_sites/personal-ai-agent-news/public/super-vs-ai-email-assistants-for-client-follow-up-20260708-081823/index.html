AI email assistants

They usually live inside the inbox. Their strengths are summarizing threads, polishing tone, extracting action items, and generating reply drafts.

  • Good for dense threads and quick drafting.
  • Weaker when the next step happens outside email.
  • Often misses SMS, browser, and deliverable context.

Super

Super is designed around the broader personal AI agent job: understand the message, choose the next action, and help execute across apps and surfaces.

  • Useful for text-message follow-up and client coordination.
  • Connects recurring browser work through computer-use memory.
  • Can turn requests into public drafts, sites, or deliverables.

Where email tools win

They are fast inside a single inbox, especially when the task is replying to a long thread without leaving email.

Where Super wins

Super is stronger when the follow-up crosses channels: SMS, calendar, browser, notes, and deliverables.

The deciding question

If the next step is just a reply, email AI may be enough. If the next step is work, use an agent layer.

Comparison checklist

  • Can it detect when a client email implies a text follow-up?
  • Can it open the right browser workflow after drafting?
  • Can it remember prior approved actions?
  • Can it create a client-facing deliverable, not just a reply?

Is Super an email assistant?

Super can support follow-up that starts in email, but the better framing is a personal AI agent control layer. It is more useful when the follow-up crosses messages, browser work, and deliverables.

When should I keep a dedicated AI email assistant?

Keep it when your needs are mostly thread summaries, tone rewrites, and reply drafts inside one inbox. Use Super when the next step requires action outside the inbox.
